EPA format excel

Project Discussion

In this project, I have incorporated at least three topics from the class: data wrangling, identification pattern, relationship, and the ability to incorporate packages. I started this project with data wrangling by making reusable codes to transform simple water quality data into an EPA excel file. Data wrangling itself is defined as the process of data cleaning, arranging, unifying messy data for easy use and access. The first purpose of this project is to facilitate python codes to easyly transform water quality data into a more organized and easy-to-access EPA format file. To ensure that the data can be written and saved into EPA format excel, I integrated a new package called openpyxl. Openpyxl is a python library that helps users read-write in Excel 2010 files. I combined panda and openpyxly library to build excel-EPA codes.

To understand the data and characterize pothole water quality and quantity dynamics, I visualized the data with seaborn, matplotlib, and correlation between each parameter. The figures show that water quality and quantity in a pothole can be a complex process with various factors involved. It is difficult to model or predict the dynamics of pothole water quantity and quality with limited resources. I attempted to apply a simple linear regression model between the maximum daily water depth and daily water quality data to understand its relationship further. However, I barely found any good relationship between them, and therefore the linear relationship is not the best option to predict water quality in the potholes based on the changes in water depth.

Overall, I have learned a lot during my project, including building an HTML website and making it pretty, incorporating a new package, and applying the topic in class to my project. My project is still far from perfect, but I have learned and practiced python codes a lot with it, and I hope you can also find it helpful.
