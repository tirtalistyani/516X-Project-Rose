Pothole Water quality

Potholes are shallow, semi-permanent, and isolated depressional area where water often stand during wet period. They are located in the northern part of the United States and some provinces in Canada due to the geological development during the deglaciation. In Iowa, these potholes have been disturbed by artificial sub-surface drainage to reduce the wetness of these depressions. Despite these changes, frequent flood events in potholes continue to occur on spring season when it is critical for crop planting.
