Pothole Water Quality

Introduction

Research about agricultural potholes is not readily available as studies mainly focus on the ecological system and wildlife of the pothole system. Yet, rainage in agricultural pothole can significantly affect the spatial and temporal dynamics of ponding in farmed prairie potholes. It can act as a recharge area for runoff water and as the concentrated location for nutrient sources, such as phosphorus and nitrate from fertilizer. Phosphoric sediments have a high potential to fill potholes, but with drainage, are provided a route that short-circuits storage and transports it downstream, contributing to algae blooms and eutrophication. Furthermore, direct water transport by the drainage, from potholes through the downstream river also increases the flooding during high precipitation events. Therefore, it is important to determine the best agricultural conservation practice that will bring the maximum benefit for farmer as well as sustainable ecosystem.

In this project I will be focusing on the water quality dynamics in the pothole and how the parameters in pothole system interacts to affect the dynamics of pothole water quality. In the year 2016 to 2018, ISU pothole team had conducted continues monitoring in pothole water quality and quantity during spring ans summer seasons in several pothole within the Des Moines Lobe. In this class project, I try to understand and visualize the results of our water quality monitoring. However, because of limited time for this project, we will only discussing the data for the year 2016.

I started this project by sorting and organizing the data with this python codes, to prepare the data for submission to the EPA. After that, I try to know the correlation between the collected water quality data per pothole. Here, I pick five potholes to be investigated, namely Walnut, Bunny, Lettuce, Mouth and Turkey. I only picked five porholes because of the availability of the data.
