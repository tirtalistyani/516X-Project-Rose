Pothole Water Quantity

Introduction

Water quality in the pothole is possibly related to the changes in water depth. As the pothole is filled with water from overland flow and a slow infiltration process, concentrated nutrients such as nitrogen and phosphorus can possibly be transported along with sediment and excess water. With that assumption, the changes in pothole water depth can also lead to the dynamics of water quality. Of course, there are a lot of factors that influence the amount of nutrients in the water, and it can be very complex. Therefore, this study aims to determine whether there is a correlation between the changes in daily water depth and water quality parameters.

Pothole water quality parameters and water depth

I apply simple linear regression into pothole monitoring data of water quality and water depth in two potholes (Bunny and Walnut). The data used in this regression is daily data, maximum daily depth and average daily water quality parameters (Ammonia-nitrogen, Soluble Reactive Phosphorus (SRP), Nitrate+Nitrite, Total Nitrogen, Total Phosphorus, Total Suspended Solids).
